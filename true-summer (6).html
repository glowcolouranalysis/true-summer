The simple rule: If it looks warm, bright, or too dark and heavy — skip it. If it looks cool, soft, and gently balanced — you are in the right place.

This is a guide, not a rulebook. Every colour in your palette can be worn anywhere. Clothing, makeup, nail polish, jewellery, eyewear, ties, hats, scarves and beyond. Use this as a reference to understand what works with your natural colouring, then trust yourself. Colour analysis is about empowerment, not restriction.

The True Summer palette contains thousands of colours. The swatches in this guide are carefully chosen examples and reference points to help you understand the qualities to look for — cool, soft, and gently muted. When you are shopping, use these as a visual compass, not a checklist. If a colour shares those same qualities, it likely belongs to you.

Screen tip: For the most accurate colour reference, turn off your blue light filter and set your screen brightness to around 80 percent or higher before browsing your palette. On iPhone, look for Night Shift under Settings > Display & Brightness. On Samsung, look for Eye Comfort Shield under Settings > Display. On other Android devices it may be called Night Light or Blue Light Filter under Settings > Display.

Online shopping tip: When shopping online and unsure about a colour, try asking an AI assistant with the brand, product, and colour name included. For example: "Is [Brand] [Product] in [Colour Name] cool or warm toned? Does it suit True Summer?" Then do a visual check by comparing the item against your palette. Use AI as a starting point — always check samples in store when possible, contact the brand directly, or ask staff for help.

Accessories & Eyewear
Your palette is your guide for every accessory — jewellery, bags, shoes, scarves, eyewear, ties, hats, nail polish and more.
The bigger picture: Every colour in your palette can be referenced for accessories. A True Summer rose bag, a powder blue scarf, a soft plum nail — it all works. The guidance below helps you understand why certain choices feel more harmonious, so you can shop with confidence rather than follow a checklist.

Pewter, antique silver, matte white gold, and platinum all mirror the cool, soft quality of your colouring beautifully. Traditional yellow gold or shiny bright gold clashes with your natural clarity. Tip: when in doubt, pewter or antique silver always works.

Smoky stones that match your palette are your most naturally harmonious choice — think smoky quartz, grey moonstone, labradorite, and muted sapphires. Pearls in white, grey, and rose are all beautiful. Soft cool gems like lavender amethyst, periwinkle sapphire, and aquamarine are deeply flattering. Avoid: amber, coral, warm citrine, or yellow gold settings.

Soft grey, charcoal, and cool taupe frames look effortlessly refined on you. Slate blue is a beautiful expressive choice. Silver and transparent cool-toned frames are also perfect. Avoid: warm tortoiseshell, golden brown, amber, or orange-toned frames.
Lens tints for sunglasses
Cool grey or soft blue-grey lenses are your most harmonious options. Soft rose or lavender-tinted lenses also work beautifully and feel very True Summer. Avoid amber, brown, or warm orange lenses — they cast a warm light that works against your colouring.

Deep charcoal and deep navy are your most powerful and versatile bases. Antique silver and soft white are elegant neutrals. Cool cocoa works beautifully for bags and shoes — look for shades with no warm red or orange undertone. Avoid: warm tan, camel, golden beige, or warm brown.
Scarves, Hats & Wraps
Near-face accessories matter most
Anything worn close to your face — scarves, hat brims, collars — has the same impact as a top or blouse. Use your Basics colours as a guide here. A powder blue scarf, a dusty rose hat, or a lavender wrap near your face will all be flattering. Tip: a cool-toned scarf near the face can transform an otherwise neutral outfit into something that truly works for your season.

Your palette is your nail guide — dusty rose, cool berry, soft mauves, lavender, and soft plum are all stunning. For a clean nail, opt for a sheer cool rose or soft pink. A soft silver or cool grey is a sophisticated neutral. Avoid: warm nude, peach, coral, orange, or brown-toned polishes.
For the Men
Ties, pocket squares & accessories
The same palette applies. Your True Summer colours translate directly into ties, pocket squares, cufflinks, and accessories. A deep navy or slate blue tie is a classic power choice. Charcoal and soft grey suits are your strongest base. Silver cufflinks over gold. A cool white shirt over cream. A French blue or periwinkle tie over brown or olive.
Makeup & Hair
Your True Summer guide to beauty. Cool, soft, and gracefully balanced. Shades listed are longstanding classics worth looking for.
Your golden rule: If it looks warm, golden, or brown-toned — put it back. True Summer makeup is cool, soft, and refined. A cool berry lip with a light smoky eye will always look more "you" than a full warm face. Elegance is effortless when you work with your natural colouring.

Your "nude" lip — cool, not beige
Traditional warm nudes wash you out. Your version of a natural lip is a sheer cool pink, soft rose, or cool mauve balm. Try: ILIA Tinted Lip Conditioner, RMS Beauty Wild Roses worn lightly, or a cool rose lip liner worn alone.
Blush
Cool pinks and soft roses bring life to your face without fighting your natural colouring. Avoid bronzer entirely — it introduces exactly the warmth your palette doesn't need.

Foundation & Tinted Moisturiser
What to look for
Ask for pink, cool, or neutral undertone in your shade. Avoid shades described as golden, warm, yellow, or honey. Tip: when testing foundation, a match that disappears means neutral. One that looks orange or yellow means it's too warm for you.
Eyes
Eyeshadow — what works
Base shades: soft white, cool rose beige, silver grey, pale lavender. Depth: cool taupe, grey, soft navy, dusty violet, charcoal. Accents: soft silver, pale lavender, dusty mauve, slate blue. Avoid: gold, bronze, warm brown, terracotta, peach, or any warm metallic.
Eyeliner
Soft charcoal or cool grey is your most versatile liner. Navy is beautiful for a soft smoky effect. Tip: a smudged grey liner with a dusty rose lip is a quintessential True Summer look.
Mascara
Black or dark navy mascara is your best choice. Brown mascara introduces warmth and dilutes your natural cool contrast.
Brows & Beard
Brow & beard guidance
Look for cool taupe or soft cool medium brown. Avoid anything warm, golden, or auburn-toned. Tip: ask for "cool" or "taupe" tones when shopping brow pencils or powders.
Hair Colour
Your best hair colours
Your naturals: cool medium brown, cool beige blonde, and soft silver tones. Always cool, never golden. Avoid: golden highlights, copper, warm auburn, caramel, or honey.
Embracing silver & grey
Silver and grey hair can be absolutely stunning on True Summer. Tip: a purple or blue toning shampoo keeps it cool and luminous rather than yellow.
What to ask your colourist
Ask for "cool toned," "no warmth," or show them your palette directly. Tip: when in doubt, go cooler and softer rather than warmer and brighter.
Shopping tip: When you find a product you love but are not sure if it works, try asking an AI assistant: "Is [Brand Name] [Product Name] in [Shade Name] cool or warm toned? Is it suitable for True Summer?" Then do a visual check comparing the product swatch against your palette. Trust your eye.
