The simple rule: If it looks warm, bright, harsh, or very dark — skip it. If it looks cool, soft, balanced, and gently refined — you're safe. Think: "Cool first, soft second."

This is a guide, not a rulebook. Every colour in your palette can be worn anywhere. Clothing, makeup, nail polish, jewellery, eyewear, ties, hats, scarves and beyond. Use this as a reference to understand what works with your natural colouring, then trust yourself. Colour analysis is about empowerment, not restriction.

Your palette is limitless. The True Summer palette contains thousands of colours. The swatches in this guide are carefully chosen examples and reference points to help you understand the qualities to look for — cool, soft, and gently balanced. When you are shopping, use these as a visual compass, not a checklist. If a colour shares those same qualities, it likely belongs to you.

Screen tip: For the most accurate colour reference, turn off your blue light filter and set your screen brightness to around 80 percent or higher before browsing your palette. On iPhone, look for Night Shift under Settings > Display & Brightness. On Samsung, look for Eye Comfort Shield under Settings > Display. On other Android devices it may be called Night Light or Blue Light Filter under Settings > Display.

Online shopping tip: When shopping online and unsure about a colour, try asking an AI assistant with the brand, product, and colour name included. For example: "Is [Brand] [Product] in [Colour Name] cool or warm toned? Does it suit True Summer?" Then do a visual check by comparing the item against your palette. Use AI as a starting point. It has limitations and pulls from what is available online, so always check samples in store when possible, contact the brand directly, or ask staff for help.

How to use this palette as a range guide: Your palette spans from soft whites and pale pastels all the way through to navy and charcoal — and all of it works for you. Use the lightest swatches as your guide to how light you can go while keeping that cool, soft quality. Use the darkest swatches as your guide to how deep you can go before a colour starts to feel heavy or harsh on you. The key is never the depth alone — it is always whether the colour stays cool and soft. A very pale colour that is warm or stark will not work. A deeper colour that is cool and muted will. Use the full grid as your personal quality reference, not a list of exact shades to match.

Accessories & Eyewear
Your palette is your guide for every accessory — jewellery, bags, shoes, scarves, eyewear, ties, hats, nail polish and more.
The bigger picture: Every colour in your palette can be referenced for accessories. A True Summer rose bag, a powder blue scarf, a soft plum nail — it all works. The guidance below helps you understand why certain choices feel more harmonious, so you can shop with confidence rather than follow a checklist.

Lens tints for sunglasses
Cool grey or soft blue-grey lenses are your most harmonious options. Soft rose or lavender-tinted lenses also work beautifully and feel very True Summer. Avoid amber, brown, or warm orange lenses — they cast a warm light that works against your colouring.

Scarves, Hats & Wraps
Near-face accessories matter most
Anything worn close to your face — scarves, hat brims, collars — has the same impact as a top or blouse. Use your Near The Face colours as a guide here. A powder blue scarf, a dusty rose hat, or a lavender wrap near your face will all be flattering. Tip: a cool-toned scarf near the face can transform an otherwise neutral outfit into something that truly works for your season.

For the Men
Ties, pocket squares & accessories
The same palette applies. Your True Summer colours translate directly into ties, pocket squares, cufflinks, and accessories. A soft navy or steel blue tie is a classic power choice. Charcoal and soft grey suits are your strongest base. Silver cufflinks over gold. A cool white shirt over cream. A French blue or periwinkle tie over brown or olive.
Makeup & Hair
Your True Summer guide to beauty. Cool, soft, and gracefully balanced. Shades listed are longstanding classics worth looking for.
Your golden rule: If it looks warm, peachy, golden, or brown-toned — put it back. True Summer makeup is cool, soft, and refined. A cool berry lip with a light smoky eye will always look more "you" than a full warm face. Elegance is effortless when you work with your natural colouring.
Lips

Your "nude" lip — cool, not beige
Traditional warm nudes wash you out. Your version of a natural lip is a sheer cool pink, soft rose, or cool mauve balm. Look for shades described as cool rose, cool mauve, or sheer berry. Try: ILIA Tinted Lip Conditioner, RMS Beauty Wild Roses worn lightly, or a cool rose lip liner worn alone.
Blush
Cool pinks and soft roses bring life to your face without fighting your natural colouring. Blush should look like a cool, healthy flush — not warmth. Avoid bronzer entirely; it introduces exactly the warmth your palette doesn't need.

Foundation & Tinted Moisturiser
Your most important foundation rule: cool or neutral undertone only. Pink-based or neutral foundations work best. Yellow or golden-based foundations will fight your natural colouring.
What to look for
Ask for pink, cool, or neutral undertone in your shade. Avoid shades described as golden, warm, yellow, or honey. For tinted moisturisers, ILIA Skin Tint and RMS Beauty "Un" Cover-Up are great natural options. Tip: when testing foundation on your wrist, a match that disappears means neutral. One that looks orange or yellow means it's too warm for you.
Eyes
Your eyes look their most beautiful with cool, softly blended shades. Think dusty lavender, cool taupe, soft grey, and gentle rose. Frosted and soft metallic finishes catch the light elegantly. Skip warm browns entirely — they dull your natural clarity.
Eyeshadow — what works
Base shades: soft white, cool rose beige, silver grey, pale lavender. Depth: cool taupe, grey, soft navy, dusty violet, charcoal. Accents: soft silver, icy lavender, dusty mauve, slate blue. Soft satin and matte finishes feel most true to the season. Avoid: gold, bronze, warm brown, terracotta, peach, or any warm metallic.
Eyeliner
Soft charcoal or cool grey is your most versatile liner. Navy is beautiful for a soft smoky effect. If black feels too sharp, opt for a cool graphite or soft dark grey. Avoid brown liner, which introduces warmth. Tip: a smudged grey liner with a dusty rose lip is a quintessential True Summer look.
Mascara
Black or dark navy mascara is your best choice. Soft charcoal works too. Brown mascara introduces warmth and dilutes your natural cool contrast. Try: ILIA Fullest Volumizing Mascara in black, NYX Worth the Hype in black, or Ere Perez Almond Oil Mascara in black.
Brows & Beard
Brow guidance
Match your brow product to your natural hair colour, or go one shade darker. Look for cool taupe or soft cool medium brown. If you have lighter hair, a cool taupe works beautifully near the face. Avoid anything warm, golden, or auburn-toned — these fight your natural cool colouring right at the frame of your face where it matters most. Tip: ask for "cool" or "taupe" tones when shopping brow pencils or powders.
Beard & grooming colour
If you colour your beard or brows, look for cooler or ashier tones. Ash brown, cool medium brown, or soft black work well. Avoid anything with a warm, reddish, golden, or auburn tone. When in doubt, go one shade cooler rather than warmer, and always do a strand test first. Tip: the same cool and ash tone guidance applies here as it does for hair colour.
Hair Colour
Your most beautiful hair is cool in tone and softly balanced in contrast. True Summer hair often naturally ranges from cool beige blonde to cool medium brown — and those natural tones are exactly what flatters you most.
Your best hair colours
Your naturals: cool medium brown, cool beige blonde, and soft silver tones. Light options: cool beige blonde or platinum — always cool, never golden. Avoid: golden highlights, copper, red tones, warm auburn, caramel, honey, or blue-black — these introduce either warmth or harsh contrast that fights your season.
Embracing silver & grey
Silver and grey hair can be absolutely stunning on True Summer. It mirrors the cool, soft elegance of your palette beautifully. If you are going silver naturally, consider leaning into it rather than covering it. Tip: a purple or blue toning shampoo keeps it cool and luminous rather than yellow.
What to ask your colourist
Ask for "cool toned," "no warmth," or show them your palette directly — having your actual cool colour reference makes the conversation easier and avoids misunderstandings. Tip: when in doubt, go cooler and softer rather than warmer and brighter.
Shopping tip: When you find a product you love but are not sure if it works, try asking an AI assistant: "Is [Brand Name] [Product Name] in [Shade Name] cool or warm toned? Is it suitable for True Summer?" Then do a visual check by comparing the product swatch against your palette. Trust your eye.
