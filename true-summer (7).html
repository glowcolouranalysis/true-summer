The simple rule: If it looks warm, bright, or too dark and heavy — skip it. If it looks cool, soft, and gently balanced — you are in the right place.

This is a guide, not a rulebook. Every colour in your palette can be worn anywhere — clothing, makeup, nail polish, jewellery, eyewear, ties, hats, scarves and beyond. Use this as a reference to understand what works with your natural colouring, then trust yourself. Colour analysis is about empowerment, not restriction.

The True Summer palette contains thousands of colours. The swatches here are carefully chosen examples and reference points — cool, soft, and gently muted. Use these as a visual compass, not a checklist.

Screen tip: For the most accurate colour reference, turn off your blue light filter and set your screen brightness to around 80 percent or higher. On iPhone look for Night Shift under Settings > Display & Brightness. On Samsung look for Eye Comfort Shield under Settings > Display.

Online shopping tip: When unsure about a colour, try asking an AI assistant: "Is [Brand] [Product] in [Colour Name] cool or warm toned? Does it suit True Summer?" Always check samples in store when possible.

Accessories & Eyewear

Anything worn close to your face has the same impact as a top or blouse. A powder blue scarf, a dusty rose hat, or a lavender wrap near your face will all be flattering. Tip: a cool-toned scarf near the face can transform an otherwise neutral outfit.

For the Men
Ties, pocket squares & accessories
The same palette applies. A deep navy or slate blue tie is a classic power choice. Charcoal and soft grey suits are your strongest base. Silver cufflinks over gold. A cool white shirt over cream. A French blue or periwinkle tie over brown or olive.

Lips
Your most flattering lip colours are cool-based and gently pigmented — soft rose, cool berry, and muted mauve feel effortless on you.
Cool Rose & Dusty Pink — your everyday lip
Marcelle Living Rose
ILIA Color Block Rosette
RMS Beauty Wild Roses
Revlon Berry Haute
Look for shades described as soft pink, cool rose, or muted mauve. These are your most wearable everyday options — cool but never stark. Tip: worn lightly, any of these give a beautiful your-lips-but-better result.
Cool Berry & Soft Mauve — polished and expressive
NYX Copenhagen
Marcelle Flirting Berry
MAC Snob
ILIA Color Block Wild Aster
Look for cool berry, soft mauve, or muted pink-plum shades. These are your go-to for work, evenings out, and any occasion where you want your lip to say something. Tip: a cool berry lip with minimal eye makeup is a quintessential True Summer look.
Soft Plum & Dusty Berry — evening depth
NARS Audacious Fanny
Charlotte Tilbury Viva La Plum
MAC Disobedient
Look for cool plum, dusty berry, or muted deep rose tones. These are your evening and occasion choices — cool depth without harshness. Avoid anything with brown, brick, or warm undertones. True Summers can wear red — look for a muted, blue-based red and apply by patting lightly rather than swiping to soften the intensity.
Your "nude" lip — cool, not beige
Warm nudes wash you out. Your version of a natural lip is a cool pink, soft rose, or sheer berry. Look for shades described as cool rose, cool mauve, or sheer berry. Try: RMS Beauty Wild Roses worn lightly, Marcelle Living Rose, or a cool rose lip liner worn alone.
Blush
Cool pinks and soft mauves bring a natural flush without warmth. Bronzer is rarely necessary for True Summer. If you enjoy wearing it, choose a cool taupe contour or a very neutral, muted bronzer — never anything warm, golden, or orange.
Go-to blush shades
ILIA Multi-Stick At Last
ILIA Color Haze Before Today
ILIA Multi-Stick A Fine Romance
Physicians Formula Blushing Berry
Look for cool pink, cool rose, or muted berry blush. Avoid peach, coral, bronze, or terracotta. Tip: a multi-stick used on lips and cheeks creates a beautifully cohesive cool look.
Foundation & Tinted Moisturiser
Your most important foundation rule: cool undertone first. Pink-based foundations work best across every skin tone and depth.
What to look for
Always look for a cool undertone first — this applies across every skin tone and depth. If you can't find a perfect cool match, a cool-neutral is your next best option. Avoid anything described as warm, golden, yellow, or honey. Tip: swatch on your jawline in natural light — a match that blends seamlessly is right. One that pulls orange, red, or yellow is too warm.
Eyes
Your eyes have real room to play in the blues, purples, and cool greys. A soft eye works beautifully with a bolder lip.
Eyeshadow — what works
Base shades: soft white, cool rose beige, silver grey, pale lavender. Depth: cool taupe, smoky mauve, grey, soft navy, dusty violet, charcoal. Accents: soft silver, pale lavender, dusty mauve, slate blue. Avoid: gold, bronze, warm brown, terracotta, peach, or any warm metallic.
Eyeliner
Soft charcoal, cool grey, slate, or navy are your most versatile liners — each giving a different softness and depth. Tip: a softly smudged charcoal liner with a dusty rose lip creates an effortlessly elegant, understated finish.
Mascara
Black, dark navy, or cool dark brown mascara are your best choices. Warm brown mascara can introduce unwanted warmth — cool dark brown, charcoal, or navy all work beautifully depending on your contrast level.
Brows & Beard
Brow & beard guidance
Match your brow product to your natural hair colour or go one shade darker. Look for cool taupe, cool medium brown, or cool dark brown depending on your depth. Avoid anything warm, golden, auburn, or reddish-toned. Tip: ask for "cool" tones when shopping brow pencils or powders.
Hair Colour
Your most beautiful hair colour is often the one you were born with. True Summer naturally spans from lighter cool blondes through to deeper cool browns.
Your best hair colours
Your naturals: cool blonde through cool medium brown and cool dark brown — the full range works as long as the tone stays cool. A cool or neutral chocolate brown can work beautifully, provided it doesn't pull warm, red, or golden. Avoid: golden highlights, copper, warm auburn, caramel, or honey.
Embracing silver & grey
Silver and grey hair can be absolutely stunning on True Summer. Tip: a purple or blue toning shampoo keeps it cool and luminous rather than yellow.
What to ask your colourist
Ask for "cool toned," "no warmth," or show them your palette directly. Tip: when in doubt, go cooler and softer rather than warmer and brighter.
Shopping tip: When you find a product you love but are not sure if it works, try asking an AI assistant: "Is [Brand Name] [Product Name] in [Shade Name] cool or warm toned? Is it suitable for True Summer?" Then do a visual check comparing the product swatch against your palette. Trust your eye.
